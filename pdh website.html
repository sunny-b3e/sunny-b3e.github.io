What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a feeling of stress, fear or worry that usally passes when the cause is removed. However, when these feelings persist, or get brought up by everyday situations it starts to affect peoples life. People who experience this can be said to have an anxiety disorder.

Causes of Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety can be caused by social and environmental factors. these can include social isolation, childhood trauma, stress related to school, work and physical or mental health. societal pressures can also affect anxiety. Some studies show that genetic could also play a role.

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety Disorders prevent people from being able to live comfortably due to persistent feelings of stress, fear or worry.

Types of Anxiety Disorders

  • Generalised Anxiety Disorder(GAD)

    GAD is characterised by constant, presistent and irrational fear of everyday occurences. This makes it hard to study, and/or interact with friends and family.

    Symptoms include:
    • Constant and excessive worry about day-to-day events such as school or work
    • Difficulty sleeping
    • Constantly feeling restless or on edge
    • Sweating

  • Social Anxiety Disorder

    Social anxiety causes people to avoid social interactions(e.g making friends) due to fear of being judged or humiliated.

    Symptoms include:
    • Sweating, blushing or trembling
    • Having "mind-blanks"
    • Difficulty speaking to strangers, making eye contact, and being in social situations.
    • Fear of judgement
    • Avioding social establishments

  • Post-Traumatic Stress disorder (PTSD)

    PTSD is an axnxiety disorder that occurs in people who have experienced a traumatic event, set of events or circumsises. This causes memories or emotions from said event to resurface when a person encounters a trigger. For example a war veteran might get triggered by a loud noise due to its semblance to the sound of a bomb.

    Symptoms include:
    • Extremely vivid flash-backs to the point of almost re-living it.
    • Nightmares about event
    • Avoiding places, activities or people that remind you of event
    • Forgetting important aspects of event as a coping mechanisim
    • Feeling distant from friends and family and struggling to maintain relationships
    • Being constantly on edge or frightened/onguard for danger.

    For more information see these websites:

    • https://www.psychiatry.org/patients-families/ptsd/what-is-ptsd#section_0
    • https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/post-traumatic-stress-disorder/symptoms-causes/syc-20355967

  • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder(OCD)

    OCD is an anxiety disorder hare due to unwanted intrusive thoughts and/or irrational fears. this leads to repetitive routine behaviours as a way to cope with their anxiety called compulsions. For example, someone with an irrational fear of germs might feel the need to constantly wash their hands or avoid touching certain objects.

    Symptoms:
    • Inability to control their compulsions or obsessions, despite knowing its excessive.
    • Spending more than 1 hour a day on their compulsions or obsessions
    • May experience a temporary feeling of relief from anxiety from compulsions but recives no personal pleasure or enjoyment.
    • Experiencing hinderences to everyday life due to obssesions and compulsions.

    For more information see this website: https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/topics/obsessive-compulsive-disorder-ocd

  • Panic Disorder

    Panic Disorder is when a person experiences frequent, disabking panic attacks. Having a single panic attack does not qualify you for panic disorder.

    Symptoms of panic disorder include:
    • Having unexpected and recurring panic attacks.
    • Constant worry or fear over having another panic attack.
    • Avoidaning things/places that trigger panic attacks.
    Symptoms of a panic attack include:
    • Sweating
    • Trembling/shaking
    • Racing heart
    • Shortness of breath/feeling like your choking
    • Nausea/dizziness
    • Chest pain
    • Stomach pain

  • Agoraphobia

    People with agoraphobia feel fear when having to leave an evironment they are familiar with or consider to be safe. In sever cases, a persons home may be the only safe environment, causing them to aviod leaving their houses for days, months or even years.

    Symptoms include:
    • Feeling anxiety in respomse to having to leave a safe environment.
    • Fear of leaving home alone.
    • Fear of crowds/public trensportation.
    • Panic attacks
    • Requiring a companion to leave the house.

Getting Diagnosed

If you are feeling some of the symptoms mentioned above it is imortant to get an official diagnosis first before making official claims. However, if professional diagnosis is not possible it is important to do more research before self diagnosis.

Doctors will ask questions about your anxiety symptoms and how they affect your day-to-day life. a detailed questionier may be used to do this. It is important to make your responses as detailed as possible as this will help getting an accurate diagnosis. You may get diagnosed with an anxiety disorder if your symptoms affect your social life, work or school. Diagnostic decisions are made using the DSM-5 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders). This handbook is used by health proffesionals to identify and diagnoses mental illness.

Online Support

Online help may be suitable for mild axnxiety or people who may be unable/uncomfortable with face-to-face help. Some online helplines for teenagers/young adults include:

  • Beyond Blue:

    An independnet non-profit organisation that provides free online help for people with anxiety, depression and other mental health issues.

  • The Brave Program:

    An interactive, online intervention and prevention program for youth suffering with anxiety. The program teaches behavioral strategies to cope with anxiety and worry.

  • ReachOut:

    A mobile as well as online space that allows you to anonymously share your stories and experiencies, and recieve support.

  • Headspace

    Provides information on mental health issues, allows you to join communities and provides one-to-one chats with a trained clinician.

  • MoodGym

    Uses cognitive behavioral therapy and an intereactive program to practise skilles that can help prevent and manage anxiety and depression. Conitive behavioral therapy changes the way people think in order to help them feel better.

  • Mental Health Online

    Provides interactive self-help programs for anxiety and depression. Mental health online also provides therapist support as part of their program.

Supporting People with Anxiety

Seeing a friend or family member struggle with anxiety can be hard, so here are something you can do help.

  1. Start the Conversation

    Often times people suffering with mental health issues fell isolated and alone, hence the importance of lettign them know that you are there for them. Sometimes that means simply listening to how they feel as oppossed to giving advice.

  2. Try to understand

    Researching about the mental health issue they are suffering can also help, as it gets you more familiar with their symptoms and certain ways to help that may be specific to their condition.

  3. Ask how to help

    The person you wish to supprt may already know the type of help they need, so having this conversation is extremly important.

  4. Support them to seek help

    If possible encouraging them to seek professional help might be best if you feel that the situation is gettig out of hand.
