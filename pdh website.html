What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a feeling of stress, fear or worry that usally passes when the cause is removed. however, when these feelings persist, or get brought up by everyday situations it starts to affect peoples life. people who experiance this can be said to have an anxiety disorder.

Anxiety can be caused by social and environmental factors. these can include social isolation, childhood trauma, stress related to school, work and physical or mental health. societal and social pressures can also affect anxiety. some studies show that genetic could also play a role.

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety Disorders prevent people from being able to live comfortably due to persistent feelings of stress, fear or worry.

Types of Anxiety Disorders

  • Generalised Anxiety Disorder(GAD)

    GAD is characterised by constant, presistent and irrational fear of everyday occurences. this makes it hard to study, or and interact with friends and family.

    Symptoms

    syptoms include:

    • constand and excessive worry about day-to-day events such as school or work.
    • difficulty sleeping
    • constantly feeling restless or on edge
    • sweating

  • Social Anxiety Disorder

    social anxiety causes people to avoid social interactions(e.g making friends) due to fear of being judged or humiliated.

    Symptoms:

    symptoms include:

    • sweating, blushing or trembling
    • having "mind-blanks"
    • difficulty speaking to strangers, making eye contact, and being in social situations.
    • fear of judgement
    • avioding social establishments

  • Post-Traumatic Stress disorder (PTSD)

    PTSD is an axnxiety disorder that occurs in people who have experienced a traumatic event, set of events or circumsises. this causes memories or emotions from said event to resurface when a person encounters a trigger. for example a war veteran might get triggered by a loud noise due to its semblance to the sound of a bomb.

    Symptoms:

    Symptoms include:

    • extremely vivid flash-backs to the point of almost re-living it.
    • nightmares about event
    • Avoiding places, activities or people that remind you of event
    • forgetting important aspects of event as a coping mechanisim
    • feeling distant from friends and family and struggling to maintain relationships
    • being constantly on edge or frightened/onguard for danger.

  • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder(OCD)

    OCD is an anxiety disorder hare due to unwanted intrusive thoughts and/or irrational fears. this leads to repetitive routine behaviours as a way to cope with their anxiety called compulsions. for example, someone with an irrational fear of germs might feel the need to constantly wash their hands or avoid touching certain objects.

    Symptoms:

    symptoms:

    • inability to control their compulsions or obsessions, despite knowing its excessive.
    • spending more than 1 hour a day on their compulsions or obsessions
    • may experience a temporary feeling of relief from anxiety from compulsions but recives no personal pleasure or enjoyment.
    • experiencing hinderences to everyday life due to obssesions and compulsions.

Getting Diagnosed

If you are feeling some of the symptoms mentioned above it is imortant to get an official diagnosis first before making official claims. However, if professional diagnosis is not possible it is important to do more research before self diagnosis.

Doctors will ask questions about your anxiety smptoms and how they affect your day-to-day life. a detailed questionier ma be used to do this. it is important to make your responses as detailed as possible as this will help getting an accurate diagnosis. you may get diagnosed with an anxiety disorder if your symptoms affect your social life, work or school. Diagnostic decisions are made using the DSM-5 (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders). This handbook is used by health proffesionals to identify and diagnoses mental illness.
